Best baseball gloves for 2026

Quick answer

The Rawlings Pro Preferred is the best baseball glove for 2026 thanks to its superb steerhide leather and handcrafted feel.

The Wilson A2000 remains a top runner-up for infielders who want a faster break-in, while the Mizuno Pro Select wins the editor's pick for its traditional kip leather and timeless design.

A baseball glove is your most personal piece of gear, it shapes how you field, catch, and transfer. Leather quality, web style, and pattern dictate break-in time and longevity, so choosing the right one matters more than any other piece of equipment. To pick the best glove for your position and budget, focus first on leather type (full-grain steerhide lasts longest but takes the most work, cowhide breaks in quickly, and synthetic is lighter but less durable). Next, match the web style to your position, an I-web or H-web for infielders, a trapeze or basket web for outfielders. Finally, consider fit and closure; a snug hand stall gives you better control, while a wider opening can accommodate a padded batting glove underneath.

Best for specific needs

Best for infielders who need a fast break-in

Middle infielders rely on quick glove-to-hand transfers and a shallower pocket for ball scooping. The Wilson A2000 excels here because its Pro Stock leather breaks in noticeably faster than steerhide, and its patterns are designed specifically for turning double plays. The H-web and basket web options give you the flexibility to choose what feels best for your hand. Our pick: Wilson A2000 Baseball Glove.

Best for outfielders who want a deep, secure pocket

Outfield gloves need a larger surface area and a deeper pocket to secure fly balls, especially when running at full speed. The Rawlings Pro Preferred offers generous pattern sizes and a hand‑stitched pocket that, once broken in, holds the ball firmly. The steerhide leather also provides extra support when you have to reach up or dive. Our pick: Rawlings Pro Preferred Baseball Glove.

Best for budget-conscious players and youth beginners

Not everyone needs a top‑end pro glove, and the Rawlings Player Preferred gives you a genuine Rawlings glove at a very accessible price. It’s soft out of the box, so young players can start catching right away without months of shaping. While it won’t last as long as premium models, it is more than capable for Little League, practice, or weekend pickup games. Our pick: Rawlings Player Preferred Baseball Glove.

Our verdict

No single glove works for every player, but the Rawlings Pro Preferred stands out as the best all-around choice for 2026 if you are willing to invest the time and money. For infielders who want a quicker break-in, the Wilson A2000 is a proven alternative. The Mizuno Pro Select appeals to glove lovers who value feel, the Rawlings Player Preferred covers budget needs, and the Wilson A900 sits at the very top for those who want the absolute finest materials. Focus on your position, break-in patience, and budget, and you will find the right glove to take the field with.
